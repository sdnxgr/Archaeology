I had a lot of time during COVID in 2020 to attempt the creation some Munsell gpl palettes for QGIS, first for the standard soil set we use in archaeology, and ultimately for all Munsell colors.
Since I'm working with them again, I thought I'd share for anyone interested.

The conversion is based on a paper by Paul Centore: 2013. Conversions Between the Munsell and sRGB Colour Systems. I don't have info on the publication/journal it came from.
All conversions of Munsell colors to sRGB are based on his paper.
My conversion of his sRGB values to .gpl palettes was accomplished in spreadsheet format.


The file names should be self-explanatory:

Munsell All.gpl is just that. All Munsell values including soils.
Munsell Neutral Grays.gpl is not included in the soils chart, but is part of the Munsell system and is separate here.
Munsell Soils Standard.gpl is limited to soil colors only.
Munsell Soils Standard Chroma.gpl is the same but sorted by chroma values.

Individual Hues includes all Munsell soil colors separated into individual hue .gpl files. Not necessary to install if you have the other ones installed already, but included here anyway.

Obviously matching digital sRGB colors to actual Munsell colors is going to suffer from numerous environmental effects, including monitor color settings, lighting, etc. 
These palettes are meant to be best approximations to assist in visualizing soil differences in profiles/sections or horizontal soil changes. No implication of accuracy is presented here when compared to original Munsell palettes.
